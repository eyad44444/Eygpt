My favorite food in Eygpt

Kashari

Koshari is Egypt's beloved national dish, a hearty, plant-based comfort food layering rice, macaroni, and lentils, topped with chickpeas, a tangy tomato-vinegar sauce, garlic, and crunchy fried onions, offering a unique mix of textures and bold flavors, making it an iconic and affordable staple found from street carts to restaurants across the country.

Fool W Tammaya

Foul (pronounced "fool") and ta'ameya are the quintessential Egyptian street food pairing and a beloved national breakfast , enjoyed from bustling Cairo streets to family tables across the country. This humble, hearty, and entirely vegan meal offers a satisfying start to the day, packed with protein and history stretching back to ancient times, Foul medames is a rich, slow-cooked stew of fava beans, traditionally simmered in a large pot for hours and seasoned simply with olive oil, lemon juice, garlic, and cumin. It's typically served warm, often with the beans lightly mashed, and can be customized with various toppings like chopped tomatoes, onions, parsley, or a drizzle of tahini sauce, Ta'ameya is Egypt's distinctive take on falafel, a crispy deep-fried patty made from ground fava beans (rather than the chickpeas used in most other Middle Eastern falafel) mixed with fresh green herbs and spices like parsley, coriander, and dill. The result is a lighter, fluffier fritter with a vibrant green interior, Served together, usually scooped up with fresh baladi bread, foul and ta'ameya create a balanced and delicious experience, often accompanied by a simple salad and an assortment of tangy pickles. This iconic duo is more than just food; it's a shared cultural experience that unites people from all walks of life in Egypt,
